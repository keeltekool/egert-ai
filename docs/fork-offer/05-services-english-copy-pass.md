- less oppositional than "you do not need another AI workshop";
- less US-style "world's best" energy;
- more Estonian-market restraint;
- still clearly commercial;
- easy to translate into natural Estonian later.

The page should sound like a serious builder who can consult, train, and build - not like another AI consultant landing page.

## How it works

### Heading

How the work runs

### Intro

Direct, scoped, and visible. You work with the person doing the work, not through an agency layer.

### Step 01

#### Title

Start with the problem

#### Body

You describe the goal, the process, and where things currently break down. I ask specific questions about users, data, constraints, and what a useful first version would need to do.

### Step 02

#### Title

Agree the shape

#### Body

Before work starts, we agree what is in scope, what is not, and what the next useful step should produce. That might be a recommendation, a training session, or a working product.

### Step 03

#### Title

Work in the open

#### Body

For a build, you see the product as it develops. The goal is to catch unclear assumptions early, not wait until the end to discover that something important was missed.

### Step 04

#### Title

Hand over cleanly

#### Body

The end state is clear ownership: code, deployment, keys, documentation, and the reasoning behind the solution. The point is to leave you with a working system, not dependence.

## Translation notes for the later Estonian pass

These English lines were chosen because they can become natural Estonian without awkward literal phrasing:

- "From AI discussion to working software." -> "AI-jutust töötava tarkvarani."
- "Start with a conversation." -> "Alustame vestlusest."
- "Clear judgment before you spend time or money building." -> "Selgus enne, kui hakkad aega ja raha ehitamisse panema."
- "Software built around the workflow, not around the buzzword." -> "Tarkvara töövoo järgi, mitte märksõna pärast."
- "You work with the person doing the work." -> "Suhtled inimesega, kes töö ka ära teeb."

Avoid translating these literally later:

- "the thing built";
- "not mockups, not demos";
- "working software, not slide decks";
- "AI readiness";
- "unlock productivity";
- "boost your business with AI."

They either feel imported, overused, or too slogan-like for the market tone we want.
