# Codex content-only output: Estonian AI market and Egert offer page

_Compiled July 9, 2026. Scope: research, positioning, and page content only. No implementation._

## 1. Executive conclusion

The current Egert page proves one thing very well: Egert can ship real software fast. It does not yet make the commercial intent unavoidable.

The Estonian AI market is crowded with trainers, consultants, AI-readiness advisors, and automation agencies. Most of them sell one of four things:

1. AI awareness and team training.
2. AI strategy, audits, and roadmaps.
3. Workflow automation or agents.
4. Community, events, and confidence for non-technical people.

Egert's strongest position is different:

> Ma ei müü AI-koolitust. Ma ehitan valmis selle tarkvara, mida te pärast koolitust niikuinii vajaksite.

That is the entire fork-page thesis.

The new fork should not be "Egert, the AI influencer" or "Egert, the AI consultant." It should be:

> Egert Väinaste - AI-native product builder for Estonian companies that need working software, not another workshop.

This matters because the market's proof is mostly logos, training counts, testimonials, and broad promises. Egert's proof is unusually concrete: named, live, clickable products with real infrastructure, auth, payments, data pipelines, and deployed workflows.

## 2. Current page diagnosis

Current live page: https://egert-ai.vercel.app/

The current page has strong proof:

- "45 production apps. Six months."
- Live selected projects: Hinnavaht, Keeletärk, SongDrop Pro, Saku Tenniseklubi, HankeRadar, Mindloop, Keepr, Poland Travel Companion.
- A technical stack that proves production breadth.
- A short "Looking for a builder?" paragraph.

The issue is hierarchy, not substance.

Right now the page reads primarily as:

> Look what I built.

The fork page needs to read as:

> This is what I can build for you, and here is why you should choose me instead of an AI trainer, consultant, or agency.

The proof is already there. The missing layer is commercial packaging.

## 4. Market patterns

### Pattern 1: Everyone says "practical"

"Praktiline" appears everywhere. It no longer differentiates.

Better for Egert:

> Praktiline tähendab minu jaoks seda, et asi töötab päris kasutajate käes.

### Pattern 2: Most proof is borrowed trust

The common proof format is:

- client logos,
- number of trained participants,
- testimonials,
- founder CV,
- partnership badges,
- "we helped companies."

Egert's better proof format:

- live product URLs,
- named projects,
- technical specifics,
- production constraints,
- tests, auth, payments, data pipelines,
- handover stories.

### Pattern 3: The category is mostly "learn AI"

Most offers assume the buyer wants capability-building:

- teach the team,
- build internal AI literacy,
- run a workshop,
- create a roadmap,
- start a 90-day plan.

Egert should target a different buying moment:

> You already know enough to want the result. You do not need a course first.

### Pattern 4: Agencies sell "AI" as the object

Many sites sell "AI adoption", "AI agents", "AI workflows", or "AI transformation."

Egert should sell the business artifact:

- booking system,
- customer portal,
- internal dashboard,
- data monitor,
- SaaS MVP,
- language tool,
- automation-backed admin system.

The page should treat AI as the build advantage, not the product.

### Pattern 5: Pricing is usually hidden, except training products

Training providers often show prices. Implementation agencies usually hide them.

Egert can differentiate by showing indicative build ranges:

- Landing/site with backend: from 1,500 EUR.
- Internal tool/dashboard: from 3,500 EUR.
- AI feature with guardrails: from 2,500 EUR.
- SaaS/product MVP: from 7,000 EUR.

These numbers should be validated by Egert before publishing, but the recommendation is to show numbers. Hidden pricing makes him look like every agency. Indicative pricing makes him look direct and serious.

## 5. Strategic positioning

### Recommended category

Do not position as:

- AI consultant,
- AI trainer,
- AI influencer,
- vibe-coding coach,
- automation agency.

Position as:

> AI-native product builder.

Estonian version:

> AI-native tooteehitaja.

More buyer-friendly Estonian version:

> Ehitan AI abil valmis päris tarkvara: sisetööriistad, portaalid, dashboardid ja MVP-d.

### Recommended one-line pitch

English:

> I use AI to build production software for small teams: fast, direct, and without agency overhead.

Estonian:

> Ehitan AI abil väikestele tiimidele päris tarkvara: kiiresti, otse ja ilma agentuuri kihita.

### Recommended main argument

English:

> Estonia has plenty of people who will teach your team AI. I am for the moment after that: when you need the actual product built.

Estonian:

> Eestis on palju inimesi, kes õpetavad su tiimile AI-d. Mina olen selleks hetkeks pärast seda - kui on vaja päriselt töötav asi valmis ehitada.

### Stronger, sharper version

English:

> You do not need another AI workshop. You need the tool that workshop is supposed to lead to.

Estonian:

> Sul ei ole vaja järjekordset AI-workshoppi. Sul on vaja tööriista, milleni see workshop niikuinii peaks jõudma.

## 6. Recommended fork-page structure

The current page should stay as the portfolio. The fork page should be a sales/offer page.

Recommended section order:

1. Hero: commercial intent.
2. Contrast: training/advice vs build.
3. Proof wall: live shipped apps.
4. Offers: what I build for you.
5. Process: how a build works.
6. Why me: PM + builder + production standard.
7. Standard: stack and quality.
8. CTA: book a build call / email.

The page should not feel like a marketing landing page. It should feel like a calm, dense, credible buying page by someone who has receipts.

### 7.5 Process

**Heading**

Kuidas töö käib

**Step 1: 30-minutiline kõne**

Räägid probleemist. Ma küsin tüütult konkreetseid küsimusi: kes kasutab, kui tihti, mis praegu katki on, mis peab esimese versiooniga kindlasti töötama.

**Step 2: Scope ja hind**

Saad konkreetse ettepaneku: mida ehitame, mida mitte, palju maksab, mis ajaks esimene töötav versioon valmis on. Ei mingit lõputut tunnipõhist udu.

**Step 3: Ehitan ja näitan jooksvalt**

Näed töö käiku deploy kaudu. Mitte nii, et kuu aega on vaikus ja siis tuleb "üllatus". Kui midagi peab muutuma, saame sellest kiiresti aru.

**Step 4: Handover**

Lahendus jääb sulle: kood, deploy, keskkonnamuutujad, dokumentatsioon, kasutusloogika. Eesmärk ei ole sind sõltuvusse jätta, vaid asi tööle saada.

## 9. UI/UX content direction, not implementation

No code recommendations here, only content/layout direction for whoever builds later.

### Keep from current page

- Editorial restraint.
- White/cream surface and amber accent.
- Dense project descriptions.
- Real technical specificity.
- "Selected work" as proof, but reframe it as commercial evidence.

### Change for fork

- Hero CTA must point to contact/build call, not "See work."
- Introduce a contrast section before projects.
- Move "45+ apps" from pure brag to buyer reassurance.
- Add offer packages before final contact.
- Make prices visible if Egert is comfortable.
- Use fewer generic AI terms, more nouns buyers understand: portal, booking, dashboard, admin, monitor, payment flow, pipeline.

### Avoid

- Calling competitors charlatans publicly.
- Listing competitor names in the page copy.
- Becoming another "AI consultant" page.
- Generic claims like "boost productivity with AI."
- Overusing "AI agent" unless the offer is actually an agent.
